My Projects

Cellular Automata: Hexagonal Conway's Game of Life

Overview: This variant of Conway's Game of Life explores how altering the traditional square grid to a hexagonal one affects emergent behaviors. Utilizing p5.js for graphics, this project demonstrates the flexibility and complexity of cellular automata.

Successes and Discoveries: Incorporating a hexagonal grid introduced unique challenges but also led to the discovery of stable organisms with varying periods (2, 3, 4, and 5). This adjustment only required a minor change to neighbor calculation, illustrating the robustness of Conway's rules.

Challenges: Adapting Conway's Game of Life to a hexagonal grid tested my problem-solving skills, particularly in accurately calculating neighbors based on cell position (even or odd columns).

Greedy Path Finder

Overview: Using a greedy best-first algorithm with Manhattan distance heuristic, this simulation navigates a maze generated by Perlin noise to find the shortest path to a goal. It's akin to searching in the dark, guided by sound alone.

Successes and Discoveries: The project showcases the effectiveness of heuristics in pathfinding, creating an engaging visual demonstration of the algorithm navigating through dynamically generated mazes.

Challenges: One significant hurdle was dealing with scenarios where no path exists due to the maze layout. My ongoing task is to develop a preemptive solution to identify and handle such cases efficiently.

Brownian Motion Simulator

Overview: This project simulates Brownian motion, with one large particle influenced by collisions from numerous smaller, less massive particles. The physics involved are mostly approximations using mathematical and visual tricks to represent the phenomenon accurately.

Successes and Discoveries: While there weren't any groundbreaking discoveries, the project involved significant problem-solving, particularly in visualizing the simulation within the constraints of the p5.js library. One notable solution was to simplify the visualization by rendering only the large particle as a small rainbow particle, foregoing rendering the small particles and background for efficiency.

Challenges: Visualizing the simulation proved challenging due to the complex interactions and the need for efficient rendering. A noteworthy challenge was to accurately represent the dynamics of Brownian motion while maintaining performance within the framework's limitations. Ongoing efforts include refining the visualization and optimizing performance further.
